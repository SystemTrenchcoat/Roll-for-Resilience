# Description
Three small black bags lie on a small table. The bags are labeled A, B, and C with golden embroidery. In front of each bag sits a small piece of parchment, with text messily scribbled on them in black ink. [[Mr. Whatzit]] exclaims
> *"A prize is hidden in one of these bags; open the correct one at it is yours to keep. But be warned, these bags are a bit mischievous. Two of them are chronic liars!"*

## Statements:
- Bag A: The prize is in this bag
- Bag B: The prize is not in bag A
- Bac C: The prize is not in this bag
## Rules:
1. Bags are *nearly* identical to each other, minus their label and statement.
	- Bags can be investigated. A difficult skill check will reveal that Bag C is slightly heavier then the other two.
2. Once a bag is opened, the game is over. If the bag contains the key, the team wins and may use the key to open the prize box
3. Bags cannot be peaked inside prior to fully opening


>[!success]- Solution: 
> To solve, work through each case.
> - If the key is in bag A: 
>	- bag A’s statement is true, bag B’s statement is false, bag C’s statement is true
>- If the key is in bag B: 
>	- bag A’s statement is false, bag B’s statement is true, bag C’s statement is true
>- If the key is in bag C:
>	- bag A’s statement is false, bag B’s statement is true, bag C’s statement is false
>
>**Therefore, the key must be in bag C.**


## Prize: 
If the players guess and open the proper bag, they are awarded with a small stone piece of a larger sliding puzzle, [[The Wheel of Purity#[The Granite Puzzle](https //puzzel.org/en/slidingpuzzle/play?p=-Ovp593O5-VcfvdPg8Js)|The Granite Puzzle]]. The piece looks like an arrow.
