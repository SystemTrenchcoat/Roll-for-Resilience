## Description

The guards of [[Nervios]]

- Patrolling the city 24/7 on a schedule
    
- Guards move around in groups 
    
- Respected by the general population, but also slightly feared
    
-   
    
- Different types of guards
    

- Internal Guards:
    

- Less trained than other branches of military with less powerful weaponry
    
- Underground tunnel system connecting towers
    
- Guard towers around the city on the walls
    

- Towers can’t be seen into from the outside
    
- Citizens don’t know if a tower is manned or not
    

- Guard towers throughout the interior of city
    

- Underground tunnels connecting them
    

- Maybe they have eyes that impose crippling fear on those who don't belong? (Poisoned in 5e)
    

- Allows for fast deployment of guards to any point in the city
    
- Outsiders can’t see in – acts as a panopticon
    

- Maintains the peace, even if a guard isn’t in that specific tower
    

- Nicknamed “Eyes” by locals/rebellion
    
- Tiers of guards:
    

- Junior
    
- Senior
    
- Captain 
    

- Some loyal to the King, some the Government, some the rich, sizable amount are loyal to anyone who pays money


- Army
    

- Snipers
    
- Infantry
    

- Navy
    

- Warships
    

  

- Watchman:
    

- Guard the walls and docks from outside threats
    
- Controls who enters/exits the cities
    
- Thoroughly inspects incoming/exiting cargo 
    

- Corrupted?
    

- Some guards might use their status and position to disobey laws or take advantage of others. 
    

- Creates resentment and fear of all guards
    
- PLOTLINE: Players start as junior guards and witness a fellow guard breaking the law. They are put into a position where they must decide between what's morally right, and the approval/protection of their peers.
    

  

Damaged old tower which falls apart/players explore and find out that not all towers are functional.
