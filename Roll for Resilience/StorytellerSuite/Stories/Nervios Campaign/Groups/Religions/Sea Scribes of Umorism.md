# Description
___
For centuries, Umorism reigned as the core religion of [[Nervios]]. Practitioners of animism, Umorists believe that all things natural possess some spiritual essence. Instead of gods and deities, Umorists worship nature and specifically the sea. They believe that the Sea tests and challenges humanity in order to push them forward and elicit growth. 
## Goals
They hope to one day reintroduce Umorism back to Nervios and rekindle the passion of challenge into humanity again.
### Beliefs
Followers of Umorism believe that the Sea is divine and that it observes humanity's movements and tests their ingenuity and persistence in the face of great odds. During these great odds, humanity is pushed to develop and improve both technologically, and as a species. Mankind is expected to push forward and innovate during times of duress and failure is seen as a necessary step towards success.

# Structure
___
Umorism lacks a distinct or discernable hierarchy. All life is seen to be equal, even those who can communicate with the spirits. Everybody is expected to work together to share knowledge, create new experiences, and grow.
## Sea Scribes
Shamans of Umorism, the Sea Scribes are mediators between the Sea and man. Their job was to interpret the sea, tides, and storms in order to assist safe trade and travel. Their knowledge and abilities come from surviving the trials of the sea and ancestral wisdom passed down for generations.

# History
___
While Umorism was the head religion of Nervios for centuries, it became banned and outlawed after the rise of [[Church of Aequorism|Aequorism]] and the [[Guards|City Guard]]. Today, the ancient ways of Umorism are practiced in secrecy by certain [[Rebels]], Noble Families, and criminals.
