## Description
As players progress through the Western Tunnel from [[Central Cavern]], the path narrows and becomes increasingly more winding with multiple forking paths. Eventually, all forking paths come to a dead end or lead back onto the main path, which eventually leads to a small chamber.

In this chamber, three open doorways are carved into the wall to the left of the entrance. Despite the chamber appearing naturally formed, the doorways are clearly man-made and carved by tools. Looking through any of the doorways reveals a large grid like pattern ahead, with different plants at the intersections of row and columns. The tunnels themselves are dark, minus the pulsing glow emanating from several of the plants.

In front of the far wall opposite the entrance stands three ornate pedestals. A key slot is carved into the center column of each pedestal. A different plant sits atop each pedestal. Descriptions of the plants are shown below:

| Plant Name            | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Great Blue Cave Plant | A large plant with glowing blue leaves sit atop the pedestal. An engraving on the cap of the pedestal reads:<br>"**The Great Blue Cave plants.<br>guiding us on the right path<br>through the darkest nights.**" |
| The Seafarers Herb    | A large plant with five big turquoise leaves sit atop the pedestal. An engraving on the cap of the pedestal reads:<br>"**A turquoise hue. Rough and smooth<br>like waves, move straight through.**"              |
| Yellow Noble Blooms   | A medium-sized plant with spiky orange and green leaves sits atop the pedestal. An engraving on the cap of the pedestal reads:<br>"**sweet, sticky, sharp leaves. Mirrors<br>of the glowing blue.**"             |

A wooden sign is hung on the wall above the pedestals. The sign reads:
> **Three keys to seek out
> Three pathways to trace them out
> Three seals to shatter**

To the right of the pedestals, a square stone shrine juts from the ground. The shrine is weathered and cracking; its once intricate design beginning to fade from age. Atop the shrine rests a sculpture of a ship crashing against a large wave. A man defiantly stands at the bow of the ship holding a book and sextant. The front face of shrine has a visibly more recent addition: an image of the sealed city gate of [[Nervios]], with in opening created in the center of the rubble.
>[!info] History Check
>- If the party has already visited the [[Rebellion Mural]], they will recognize the image as the same.
>- If members of the party are involved with the rebels or the criminal underbelly of [[Nervios]], a normal-difficulty history check will reveal the symbol as the [[Rebellion Symbol]]

If the players choose to enter any of the three doorways, they begin the [[Flower Puzzle]].
## History
Upon closer inspection, the three doorways seem man-made, juxtaposing the natural formation of the rest of the tunnels and caverns.
