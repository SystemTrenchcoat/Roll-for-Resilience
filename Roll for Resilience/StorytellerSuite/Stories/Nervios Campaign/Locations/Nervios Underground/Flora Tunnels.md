# Description
___
Coming from [[Central Cavern]], the party enters a small rectangular chamber. In the far corner of the chamber, three pedestals and a stone shrine flicker in the light from adjacent torches. A sign hangs on the wall over the pedestals. To your left, three doorways are carved into the walls, their paths dim and dark. The chamber is dimly lit, the only light coming from the two torches adjacent to the shrine.
### Pedestals
Three ornate pedestals stand adjacent before you, each with a key slot carved into their center column. Atop the cap of each pedestal lies a unique and distinct plant, which appear as an example rather than an offering. The base of the pedestals are thick and are engraved with a clue pertaining to their individual plants.

### Sign
Hanging on the wall behind the pedestals, an old wooden sign hangs lopsidedly by a single frayed string. In fading black ink, barely legible, the sign reads:
> **Three locks seal the shrine
  three keys, one down each pathway
  thy gift sealed inside**

### Shrine
A square stone shrine stands a few steps behind the plant pedestals. The shrine is weathered and cracking; it's once intricate design fading and crumbling from age. A familiar icon is painted in thick black ink on the front face of the shrine: the Nervios city gate, once sealed with boulders and rubble, now open with a path cleared through the blockage. 

>[!info] Symbol History Check
> - Players familiar with the [[Rebels]] or the criminal underbelly of Nervios could recognize this symbol as the [[Rebellion Symbol]].
> - If the players have already seen the [[Rebellion Symbol]] at the [[Rebellion Mural]] or the [[Rune Tunnels]], they will recognize the symbol immediately.

A small sculpture sits in the center of the shrine atop a small pillar. The sculpture shows the image of a ship crashing into a large wave. A man stands defiantly at the bow of the ship, holding a scroll in one hand and a sextant in the other.

> [!info] Sculpture Investigation
> - Characters with connections to the [[Sea Scribes of Umorism]] can find solace with this sculpture. The ancient religion of [[Nervios]] has yet to be completely forgotten.
> - A *difficult* investigation check can reveal a small crease in between the ship and the wave. A major success can reveal that the two pieces are completely separate, and meant to split apart. 
> - If a player attempts to destroy the sculpture, they can skip the puzzle and collect the [[#Prize]] directly from within the shrine. Refer to [[#Prize]] for a relevant description.
### Doorways
Three doorways are carved into the wall to the left of the entrance. Peering into each doorway, a different plant is visible just a short pace ahead. The leftmost doorway has a [[Great Blue Cave Plant]] a few paces ahead. The center doorway has a [[The Seafarers Herb|Seafarers Herb]]. The rightmost doorway has a [[Yellow Noble Bloom]]. Not much can be seen beyond the plants as the paths grow too dark too quickly.
# Flower Puzzle
___
This chamber contains a spatial puzzle involving following a set sequence. Players must navigate a grid and follow a set path determined by different plants growing at the intersections. Each of the three doorways has a unique path to follow. The pedestals define the movement option for each plant:
> - **Great Blue Cave Plant**: When encountering this plant at an intersection, go to the right.
> - **The Seafarers Herb**: When encountering this plant at an intersection, go straight.
> - **Yellow Noble Blooms**: When encountering this plant at an intersection, go to the left (mirroring the great blue cave plant).

So if a character meets an intersection with a Great Blue Cave Plant, they should take the path to the right.

Each doors path is as follows:

| Doorway   | Solution                                                                                                                                                                                                            | Plants Encountered in Proper Order                                                                        |
| --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Leftmost  | Right -> Straight -> Left -> Straight -> Left -> Straight -> Left -> Straight -> Right -> Right -> Right -> Straight -> Left -> Straight -> Left -> Left -> Left -> Straight -> Straight -> Straight -> Left -> END | GBCP -> SH -> YNB -> SH -> YNB -> SH -> YNB -> SH -> 3X GBCP -> SH -> YNB -> SH -> 3X YNB -> 3X SH -> YNB |
| Center    | Straight -> Left -> Straight -> Right -> Right -> Left -> Left -> Right -> Right -> Straight -> Left -> Left -> Right -> Right -> END                                                                               | SH -> YNB -> SH -> 2X GBCP -> 2X YNB -> 2X GBCP -> SH -> 2X YNB -> 2X GBCP                                |
| Rightmost | Left -> Straight -> Right -> Straight -> Left -> Right -> Right -> Left -> Straight -> Right -> Left -> END                                                                                                         | YNB -> SH -> GBCP -> SH -> YNB -> 2x GBCP -> YNB -> SH -> GBCP -> YNB                                     |
If a character veers off the proper path and goes in the wrong direction, they feel a pressure plate sink beneath their feet and a trap occurs. Refer to [[#Traps]] for a random table. Each path leads to the same chamber with a round table in the center. Refer to [[#Solution]] for a description of the chamber.
## Traps
A trap occurs when a player goes down the incorrect path. Roll a D6 to determine which random trap occurs from the table below:

| Roll # | Trap                 | Effect                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ------ | -------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | Hallucinatory Spores | You suddenly feel disoriented as dizzy as the world begins to spin and warp around you. The feeling is overwhelming and forces you to close your eyes. When they reopen, you are back at the entrance doorway for which you've started. |
| 2      | Arrow Trap           | Small arrows shoot from holes in the wall near the trap. They are targeted at your feet. If they hit, take a small amount of damage.                                                                                                    |
| 3      | Wave Trap            | A small gush of water spews from beneath your feet, pushing you back to the last intersection you were at.                                                                                                                              |
| 4      | Swinging Rock        | A small rock detaches from the roof and swings at you via an underground vine. If it hits, take a small amount of damage and get knocked back to the last intersection you were at                                                      |
| 5      | Ant Trap             | Your legs begin to itch as hundreds of ants crawl onto you from the ground up, coming from the pressure plate. They cause no damage and act as a warning                                                                                |
| 6      | Misplaced Rake       | You step on the teeth of a rake hidden beneath the plant. The handle of the rake swings upwards and hits you in the face. Take a small amount of bludgeoning damage.                                                                    |

## Solution
When a doorway's path is completed, the solving character finds themselves in a small chamber with a round stone table in the center. Three magical seals are etched around the edge of the table. Each of the three paths unlock a different seal on the table. The order of unlocking does not matter for each seal provides the same prize: a small wooden key. The keys should be brought back to the starting chamber and inserted and turned into the pedestals in the starting chamber.

Once all three keys have been collected and turned into the pedestal slots, a light rumbling sound begins coming from the shrine. The sculpture of the ship and wave begin to separate in the middle as a small pedestal rises from the center. On the cap of the pedestal is an intricate stone sculpture of a closed lotus flower bud. Refer to [[#Prize]] for a detailed description.

### Prize
Within the shrine lies a small stone sculpture of a closed lotus flower bud. The petals of the bud taper together to create a rain-drop shape. A deep threaded hole is carved into the bottom of the bud, in the center of the thickest part of the sculpture. 

The sculpture is one-half of the [[Stone Lotus Flower]], the key to unlocking the [[Rebellion Mural]] door.

>[!info] History and Investigation Checks
>- If the players have visited the [[Rebellion Mural]], a *normal-difficulty* investigation/history check can reveal that the sculpture is of the same craftsmanship as the lotus flower sculptures by the pond.
>	- The check can also remind the party of the lone stem without a flower at the sculptures by the pond
