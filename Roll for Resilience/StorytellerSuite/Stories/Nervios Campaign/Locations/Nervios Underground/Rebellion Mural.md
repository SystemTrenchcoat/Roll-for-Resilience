## Description
At the end of the Eastern Tunnels from [[Central Cavern]] exists a large open cavern with high ceilings and jagged walls. The air is more humid here, with a distinct smell of saltwater. A small pond sits in the center of the cavern, exuding an ethereal glow as it is lined with bioluminescent moss. Furthermore, two lit torches are hung on the far wall from the entrance, with a massive painted mural between them. As your eyes adjust to the new lighting, the mural begins to take shape: the [[Nervios]] city gate, once sealed with boulders and rubble, now open with the boulders divided down the center.
![[rebellionSymbol.png|300]]
In the opening between the rubble, four notches are carved into the wall, roughly five feet off of the ground. 
A small cavern with a large painted mural of the [[Rebellion Symbol]]. A torch hangs on both sides of the mural; they are lit and burning despite the cavern being desolate of people. A small pond sits in the center of the cavern. It's water still and clear, allowing you to see the intricate layers of eroded rock and sediment beneath it.

>[!info] Investigation Checks
>If the player characters hold connections to [[The Burrow]] or the [[Rebels]], a normal difficulty check reveals the mural as [[Rebellion Symbol|The Symbol of the Rebellion]]. 
>
>If the player characters investigate the wall notches, a normal difficulty investigation check can reveal they are key slots. A critical success on this check reveals that the mural is painted on a large circular door; it's creases filled with dirt and dust, blending in with the surrounding wall.

>[!tip]- Key Slot Explanation
> The four keys can be found throughout the tunnels. Three are found from the [[Flower Puzzle]] in the [[Flora Tunnels]], the other is found in the chest at the end of the [[Rune Puzzle]] in the [[Rune Tunnels]].
> ___
> Once each key has been inserted into the slots and turned, a deep rumble is heard as the mural wall begins shaking. Slowly, dust and dirt begin to pour out in a circle around the mural, revealing the mural is painted onto a massive circular door. The door slowly sinks back into the wall, then rolls out of way, revealing a path ahead towards the [[Eastern Tunnel System]].
## History
The paint of the mural seems to be aging with time. An investigation can reveal the mural has been here for at least two decades.
