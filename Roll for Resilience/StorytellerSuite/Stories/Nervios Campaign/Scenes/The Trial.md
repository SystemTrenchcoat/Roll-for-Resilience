# Hooks
___
*Your party has been summoned to the courthouse effective immediately to stand accused of a crime. You all must stand before a judge, council, and crowd to prove your innocence in a court where guilt is assumed.*

The exact crime at hand is determined by the Gamemaster. The accused crime should create tension and make the players uncomfortable without pushing boundaries and remaining "comfortably uncomfortable". Example crimes are shown below.
## Example Indictments
**Destruction of Central Stage**
	Your party is blamed for the collapse of the central stage during [[The Festival of Granite Rain]]. Since the stage collapsed while you were on it, you are deemed guilty as the destructor. This is a great offense to the cultural history of [[Nervios]] and is a potential act of sedition against the [[Guards]] or [[Church of Aequorism|Clergy]].
**Association with the Rebellion**
	Your party is charged with sedition and acting with the [[Rebels|rebellion]]. Your party was spotted dealing with known rebel members or frequenting known rebellion hotspots. 
**Story Specific**
	During the previous sessions, your party has committed one or more of the many [[Crimes of Nervios]].
**False Accusation**
	Your party is accused of a crime they did not commit. 
**Character Specific**
	A member of your party stands accused of a crime that occurred in their backstory before you met.

# Story Beats
___
This mission can take place at any time during the campaign. It displays the corrupt nature of the judicial system of Nervios and exposes the party to a situation where they must stand up for themselves. The below narrative beats should be followed *in order* for a cohesive judicial trial.

## 1. The Crime
When the party is settled and in a public area, they hear a distant sound of rhythmic marching and metal clanking. As the sound grows closer and louder, any crowds nearby begin to part as a group of 10 guards makes their way towards the party, surrounding them on all sides. A guard captain steps forth and declares
>"Under the watchful gaze of The Nervios Eye and Aequorin, you stand accused of a great crime and must stand before the great court of Nervios effective immediately. Any attempts to escape or fight or will be met with violence and forgoes your right to a trial."

> [!info] Insight Check
> Players can roll for insight against the guard captains words. Upon a success, you can tell the guard is lying about losing your right to a trial. He is not lying about the violence.

If the party follows with no difficulties, they will be escorted to the [[The Courthouse]] and temporarily stripped of their inventories. If they refuse to follow, the guards will initiate combat against them in an attempt to subdue and capture them, bringing them in by force.
## 2. Collecting Evidence
The party is brought to the courthouse by the guards and stood before the judge, [[Gimil Lawman]]. He sits above the party at the tallest desk in the courtroom, with a large anchor hanging from the center of the desk. Gimil's voice booms and echoes through the tall halls of the courtroom
>"You stand guilty before Aequorin and the court for your crimes of *\*insert crime here\**. Before your case, you are allotted a third of an hour to gather and collect evidence to prove your innocence. You will be escorted to the scene of the crime by the guards who brought you here, and once you return your trial will begin."

His body shifts behind the desk as he pulls a lever, and the anchor drops and slams into the floor with a deep clang. The guards from before lead you out of the courthouse and to the scene of the crime.

Refer to [[#Finding Evidence]] for the mechanics behind uncovering evidence.

## 3. Beginning of the Trial
/// *Players are escorted into the the court room and the Galleon declares the trial begun, making a gesture to his timekeeper to make a note of when the trial started. He reads off the charges pressed against the party: Sedition and Treason!* ///
## 4. Arguments
/// *Flexible in length, this is the bulk of the session, where the other members of the party make the case for or against the accused citizen.* ///
## 5. The Verdict
/// *Other than stalling to allow for new information to be found, the declaration of a trial being finished comes once all arguments have been made or time expires. A tentative silence falls over the court as Gimil delivers the final verdict, are they to walk free or suffer punishment? The fate of the accused depends on your party’s ability to make compelling arguments, but everyone else is dismissed to return to their normal lives.* ///

# Trial Mechanic
___
## Mission Timer
Every trial in this city is allotted an hour maximum, no exceptions. Any case you wish to make, any arguments delivered, any evidence - legitimate or forged - must be presented in this time slot.
## Finding Evidence
When at the scene of the crime, everyone actively participating rolls a check against a difficulty tied to the amount of people passing through the crime scene. The check for finding evidence while the trial is ongoing will be significantly easier than beforehand, however every individual’s success adds another piece of evidence. Before the trial, it's a medium / average check, and during it's an easy check.
## Making a Case
After the opening statement and crimes have been read, the repeating process starts with the prosecution making an argument with premises, evidence, and the conclusion to draw. After this, the defense responds with an argument of their own, providing contradictory evidence, claiming lack of intent, or adding justifications. Regardless of whichever side the party is on, any arguments must be roleplayed out, however skill checks may be performed to gain the information necessary to formulate them. The rules for determining the ending verdict are as follows

Keep track of two values throughout the trial, corresponding to the successes of the prosecution and the defense. When the trial concludes, roll a number of D6 equal to the number of Defense Successes. The total is the innocence score. Then roll a number of D6 equal to the number of Prosecutor Successes. The total is the guilty score. The higher value wins the trial.
