## Description
A stone sculpture of a lotus flower made of three independent pieces: a stem, a bud, and an inner bulb. The stem stands alone in the chamber with the [[Rebellion Mural]]. The [[#Bud]] and [[#Inner Bulb]] are earned at the [[Flora Tunnels]] and [[Rune Tunnels]] respectively. Once all three pieces have been earned and screwed together, they can be used to unlock the hidden mural door at the [[Rebellion Mural]].
### Bud
A stone sculpture of a closed-lotus flower bud. It's petals form the shape of a raindrop. A deep threaded hole is carved in the bottom of the bud. The bud is earned in the [[Flora Tunnels]]. Once connected to the lone [[#Stem]] in the [[Rebellion Mural]], the bud blooms and the petals open up, revealing another threaded insert in the center of the now outward-fanning petals. The [[#Inner Bulb]] can be screwed into this insert, completing the stone sculpture and making it whole once more.

### Inner Bulb
A stone sculpture of the inner-bulb of a lotus flower. The sculpture depicts a small cylinder surrounded by several delicate strips of ribboning stone. Those familiar with flower anatomy would recognize this bulb as the seedpod and surrounding stamens. On the bottom of the bulb, a short threaded rod juts out. This rod is too large to fit into the threaded insert on the bottom of the [[#Bud]]. The threaded rod connects to the center of the [[#Bud]], which is revealed only once the bud has been connected to the stem and has bloomed. The bulb is earned in the [[Rune Tunnels]]

### Stem
A long-thin cylindrical stone rod that juts from the pond in the [[Rebellion Mural]] cavern. The rod sits amongst a group of completed lotus flower structures, yet this stem is missing its bud and bulb. The top end of the rod is threaded, and can be inserted into the bottom insert of the [[#Bud]]. The stone is cool and smooth to the touch.
## History
Found in two pieces from the [[Flora Tunnels]] and [[Rune Tunnels]]. The craftsmanship is impressive; it is clear countless hours have been put into this sculpture.
