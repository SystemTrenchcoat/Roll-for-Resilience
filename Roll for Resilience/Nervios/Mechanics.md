
- System agnostic in a polyhedral set (D4,6,8,10x2,12,20)

- Light crunch. Greater focus on narrative and characters rather than complicated and deep combat mechanics
    
- Some sort of relationship/tracker
    
- Character gains xp/earns rewards for stepping outside of pre-defined comfort zones
    
- Honor System: npcs react to pcs based on how they interact with the world. If pc’s are known in a town to be rude or mischievous then npcs are less likely to interact with them in a positive light
    
- Different degrees of success and failure; failing while doing your best, succeeding while messing up.
    
- “Stress” mechanic via stats or dice that is good in small quantities but detrimental in large ones
    
- Bonuses to charisma rolls based on previous social interactions
    
- Badges to denote popularity.
    
- The "Panopticon Paranoia" Tracker: Since the Spire instills constant fear of being watched, introduce a party-wide "Paranoia" gauge. If the party acts suspiciously in the upper rings of the city, the gauge increases. If it gets too high, it acts as a negative modifier to their Stress mechanic, making it harder to shake off detrimental effects. They can lower the gauge by doing mundane tasks, visiting the underground black market, or finding "blind spots."
    
- Decatastrophizing Checks: Tie into the specific therapeutic goals mentioned in your notes. When a character's Stress reaches a detrimental level, they enter a "Catastrophizing" state where they envision the worst possible outcome, perhaps imposing disadvantage on rolls. Another player must use a social/empathy roll to help them "Decatastrophize," bringing their stress down and offering a bonding moment that boosts their Relationship Tracker.
    
- Comfort Zone Anchors: Since characters gain XP for stepping out of their comfort zones, let them define one "Anchor" (an item, a specific NPC, or a location like a smuggler's den). Interacting with their Anchor allows them to safely shed large amounts of detrimental Stress, but if the Anchor is threatened by the Guards, it forces them into a high-stakes narrative choice.
    
- The Scler Economy (Barter vs. Coin): In the upper city, transactions use Scler coins and are heavily monitored. Mechanical rule: buying items with Scler leaves a paper trail, potentially raising the Paranoia Tracker. In the underground, bartering with scavenged goods or favors doesn't raise Paranoia but requires successful Charisma/Honor checks to pull off.

- Rumors. Each player makes 3 rumors about their character, 1 good, 1 bad, 1 false. Each player is given a rumor about another when the campaign starts
