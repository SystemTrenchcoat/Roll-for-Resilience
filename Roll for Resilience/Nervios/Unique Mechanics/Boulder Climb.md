Teams of 2-6 people must work together to bring a large spherical boulder to the top of a steep hill by any means necessary. The first team to the top of the hill wins.

**Design Intention**: This game is simple to understand and relies on player teamwork and creativity to succeed..
## Rules:
- Every member of the team must participate in some way to make progress.
- If a member of the team does not participate, then the team loses some progress
- Teams can reach the top at any means necessary. This includes using strength to push the rock up the hill, using magic in some way, destroying the rock into smaller, lighter fragments, etc. Teams are not allowed to interfere with other teams.
### Progress:
The progress of the teams is tracked by the GM and ranges from 0%-100%. Non-Player teams gain 1 D20 worth of progress per round, tracked by the GM. If a non-player team critically fails, another D20 is rolled to determine progress lost. 
### Rounds
The mini-game is split into several rounds that continue until one team reaches the top. For the team of player characters, a round is split into individual turns where players must state how they are contributing.
### Turns:
On a player’s turn, they get to decide how they are contributing to the boulder climb. The success of their contribution is determined via relevant skill roll and GM discretion. The mini-game is based on cooperation, so if their contribution doesn’t help the efforts of the party, it can be seen as a failure. At the end of all player turns, if half or more of the players succeed in their skill checks, their team progresses by a value equal to the highest player roll made that round. If less than half the players succeed, the boulder loses progress equal to 1 d6 + # of failures.
### Initiative:
Players roll for initiative to determine turn order. The player party goes first in a round. Non-player teams go after in an order determined by the GM.

**Example Team Attempts**:
- A team trying to brute force the boulder up the hill by physically pushing it.
- A team tries to pull the boulder up the hill via a rope
- A team tries to shatter their boulder to carry up smaller fragments
- A team uses magic to lift the boulder up the hill
## Prize: 
The winning team of the boulder climb is given a small wooden piece of a larger sliding puzzle. The piece contains an image that looks like a “T”
