
- System agnostic in a polyhedral set (D4,6,8,10x2,12,20)

- Light crunch. Greater focus on narrative and characters rather than complicated and deep combat mechanics
    
- Some sort of relationship/tracker
    
- Character gains xp/earns rewards for stepping outside of pre-defined comfort zones
    
- **Honor System**: NPCs react to PCs based on how they interact with the world. If PCs are known in a town to be rude or mischievous then NPCs are less likely to interact with them in a positive light
    
- Different degrees of success and failure; failing while doing your best, succeeding while messing up.
    
- “Stress” mechanic via stats or dice that is good in small quantities but detrimental in large ones
    
- Bonuses to charisma rolls based on previous social interactions
    
- Badges to denote popularity.
    
- **The "Panopticon Paranoia" Tracker**: Since the Spire instills constant fear of being watched, introduce a party-wide "Paranoia" gauge. If the party acts suspiciously in the upper rings of the city, the gauge increases. If it gets too high, it acts as a negative modifier to their Stress mechanic, making it harder to shake off detrimental effects. They can lower the gauge by doing mundane tasks, visiting the underground black market, or finding "blind spots."
    
- **Decatastrophizing Checks**: Tie into the specific therapeutic goals mentioned in your notes. When a character's Stress reaches a detrimental level, they enter a "Catastrophizing" state where they envision the worst possible outcome, perhaps imposing disadvantage on rolls. Another player must use a social/empathy roll to help them "Decatastrophize," bringing their stress down and offering a bonding moment that boosts their Relationship Tracker.
    
- **Comfort Zone Anchors**: Since characters gain XP for stepping out of their comfort zones, let them define one "Anchor" (an item, a specific NPC, or a location like a smuggler's den). Interacting with their Anchor allows them to safely shed large amounts of detrimental Stress, but if the Anchor is threatened by the Guards, it forces them into a high-stakes narrative choice.
    
- **The Scler Economy (Barter vs. Coin)**: In the upper city, transactions use Scler coins and are heavily monitored. Mechanical rule: buying items with Scler leaves a paper trail, potentially raising the Paranoia Tracker. In the underground, bartering with scavenged goods or favors doesn't raise Paranoia but requires successful Charisma/Honor checks to pull off.

- **Rumors**. Each player makes 3 rumors about their character, 1 good, 1 bad, 1 false. Each player is given a rumor about another when the campaign starts

- **Favors**. Each player may choose to claim an npc owes them a favor. The GM must agree that the favor and NPC owing the player a favor is reasonable. However, the GM can then request the same of a player later on. Each player can use this ability once (or only after they have completed their favor)

- [[Backgrounds]]. Each player must choose one of the given backgrounds at character creation

- **GM Moves**. If the gameplay is reaching a lull, the GM may use any of the below moves, or add some of their own
	- Separate/bring together the players
	    
	- Put someone in a high stakes situation
	    
	- Announce problems, on-screen, off-screen, or future
	    
	- Introduce an item
	    
	- Introduce an NPC
	    
	- Give them a difficult decision
	    
	- Turn an action on them
	    
	- Reveal an unwelcome truth
    


- **Negotiations**. When engaging a character, a player may try to gleam an insight into what the other character wants (usually insight or history) (DC 10). On a success, they get information that they can use to help them, but it poses some sort of risk (like angering the other character), with an additional degree of success (15+), they get information they can use, without the risk. On a fail, they get a piece of information about the character. It can be true or false, useful or not, but the player will not know until they use it.
    
- **Gladiator**. This is a fight to be used for entertainment (like WWE). The fight is meant to be a show, so points are awarded for flashy and showy moves, as well as prolonging the fight. Points are taken away from ending a fight to early, or not being showy enough. Players can use their abilities however they wish, but should strive to please the audience. Additionally, they have access to the Boast action where they spur on the audience, gaining more points, but leaving themselves open for attack, as well as the Declare bonus action, where they can state what they're going to do and/or how it's going to go, gaining a large amount of points on a successful call, or losing them on an unsuccessful one
    
- **Fishing**. You may ride on a fishing boat in order to fish. To do so, roll a survival check (with dex if desired). On a 1-10, roll on the Small Catch Table, on a 11-20, roll on the Large Catch Table, on a 20+, roll on the Legendary Catch Table.
