## Themes
    

- "Thought crime" aka people monitor their own thoughts and feel fear despite towers being unable to see their thoughts -- only their actions
    

- Doublethink - term coined by George Orwell (author of 1984) for an indoctrination tactic in which subjects are expected to accept conflicting beliefs as truth, as a conscious effort to unconsciously hypnotize oneself
    
- Doublespeak (or algospeak as we might call it) originated in part from doublethink. Intentional disguising, distortion, or reversal of words to make the truth more palatable.

- ## Narrative Ideas

### The Watchmen's Dilemma
    

- Players begin their journey as junior guards within the city's law enforcement.
    
- The inciting incident occurs when the party witnesses a senior guard blatantly breaking the law or taking advantage of a citizen.
    
- Players are forced into a moral dilemma between doing what is right and maintaining the approval and protection of their corrupt peers.
    
- Early sessions involve standard guard duties, such as patrolling and responding to disturbances, to establish the city's oppressive status quo.
    
- As they prove themselves, they are given a morally dubious mission that introduces them to the Rebel faction.
    
- The party must choose to operate as double agents, using their guard status to map patrol routes and underground tunnels for the rebellion.
