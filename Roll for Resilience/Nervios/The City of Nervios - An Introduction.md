*[[Nervios]] is the city that you know. You grew up here and you're familiar with the comings and goings of the city. Historically closed off due to wars and economic turmoil, the city of Nervios is isolated; with its impenetrable walls cutting it off from the rest of the continent. The peninsula has most of what the city needs; protection, food, water, stability. All this under the shadow of the [[The Nervios Eye|Nervios Eye]], the great central tower rooted in the bay that acts as a guardian of the city. Watching every move in the city. You know the rules of the city by heart now; keep your eyes down, speak only in pleasantries, and guard your every thought. A small trade for the protection of the city guard and the [[The Nervios Eye]].*
# Themes
___
## A City Built on Social Anxiety
Nervios is a city that from the ground up is supposed to embody the idea of social anxiety; the guards that control the city are on constant look out for threats and dissidents, wielding gauntlets that can read surface thoughts, and neighbors that would be punished for simply knowing of a crime and not reporting it to the guards. However, the average citizen feels safe in this arrangement, fearing the outside world and the uncertainty of guard rule more than they disdain the lack of freedom they have. As the campaign continues, the player is presented with the realities of the price paid for this security, so that they can relate this to their own social anxiety and learn that working through and taking risks is worth doing something outside of their comfort zone.

## Personal Growth and Strength
Many of the challenges the players will face can be solved in a myriad of ways, and many of the mechanics encourage the players to play to their strengths. This is to encourage the players/patients to use their own strengths and knowledge to handle challenges and tasks outside of the game.

## Conflicting Morals
Like any city, [[Nervios]] is full of both good and evil, making it difficult to explicitly define "good" or "bad" groups. The [[Guards|City Guard]] is known to be ruthless and authoritarian, yet many members wish only to do good and protect the citizens of Nervios. The [[Rebels]] are generally seen as symbols of freedom and liberty, yet they align themselves with criminals who wish to self serve and cause harm to the innocent. The [[Church of Aequorism]] works with the guard and is designed to help control the populace. Despite this, many clergymen of the Church wish to genuinely help people. There is no clear "bad guy", and it is up to the player party to interpret the world as they see fit.

# Notable Groups
___
### [[Guards]]: 
The City of Nervios is a small port city state run by an authoritarian group called the guards, which prevent the entry and exit from the city except by boat. 150 years ago during a time of economic unrest caused by two neighboring countries going to war and causing the trade routes the city relied on to be disrupted. The Guards then came into control of the city using the unrest to their advantage to position as a solution to the crisis via isolating the city from the outside world to prevent the outside world from interfering with the city again. Unfortunately, the guard rules with an iron fist and uses towers and strange mind reading gauntlets to maintain control over the city and its populace. 

### [[Rebels]]:
Not many people are pleased with the direction the city has gone in, and some have organized into a silent rebellion against the guards. These rebels help maintain the burrow through smuggling in goods and maintaining the underground, while also on the look out for possible ways to decrease the influence of the guards and bring freedom and peace of mind back to the city of Nervios.

### Nobles:
[[Nervios]] was once a monarchy, but when the guards seized control, the various noble houses decided to help overthrow the king to gain more power in the aftermath. Currently, nobles use their vast wealth and influence to run most of the business in Nervios and get family members in positions of high ranking power in the guards to maintain their power in the city. Maybe noble families live in nice, one story buildings that afford them plenty of privacy and room, with the wealthiest family having an entire island to themselves.

### [[Church of Aequorism]]:
When the guards took over the city, some members of Umorism schemed to form the religion of Aequorism, which teaches reverence and attempts to placate the ocean instead of rising to its challenges. This religion found support among the guards as it taught obedience and conformity as the correct way to handle problems, which helped the guards tremendously. Currently, the church is a major power that holds massive sway over the ports as their blessings are seen as one of the only ways to safely cross the sea. 

### [[Sea Scribes of Umorism]]:
Nervios used to practice Umorism, which teaches that the sea is a greater challenger that spurs humanity forward into new innovations and discoveries, primarily through the development of new ship building techniques and technologies. When the guards took over, they outlawed Umorism as it was seen as encouraging leaving the walls of the city and promoting dubious thoughts. Now Umorism is practiced in secret, with many of the priests of Umorism, known as Sea Scribes, mainly teaching [[Rebels|Rebel]] groups ship building techniques to better smuggle in vital goods or attempting to find old ship building lore.

### [[Commonfolk]]:
The everyday people of [[Nervios]]. Not heavily involved with the guards, rebels, or churches, these citizens just wish to live their life unbothered and in peace. They are the working class: the merchants, fisherman, dockworkers, tavern owners, husbands, and wives. Sure, they've lived their entire life under constant surveillance by the [[Guards]], but that's all they know and it's certainly worth it for protection, security, and food.
