
The quality of life disparity between the upperclass and the underclass cannot be overstated. Despite this, not many citizens can consider themselves overly rich, making the handful that are noteworthy and recognizable stand-outs, and those barely scraping by easy to spot. Falling from the lips of the impoverished are cries for more privacy, personal space, and quiet. All of which fall upon deaf ears.

Though citizens and merchants alike under duress will deny their existence, if you follow the threads of idle chatter enough you’ll hear of discrete markets beneath the city, importing in forbidden goods from foreign nations. This is how daring and enterprising individuals sneak their way around the intricacies of honest trade in Nervios, sporting scraps of the latest technology, research volumes, or a simple spice from far away. Dens for these deals often require thoughtful planning, as any fool careless enough to leave the entrance easily discoverable or clearly audible from the streets will find no business, one way or another.

Aboveboard transactions in the markets of Nervios are handled through metal coins called Scler (Sklair), hexagonal pieces of bronze smoothed around the edges, with varying iconography of the tower and its eye pressed into them to indicate their values. Short as this name may be, people make an effort to shorten it further by calling them Scul, trading goods and favors between each other with them. However, you’ll hardly find scul changing hands below the streets, as those dealing in the forbidden have learned to operate off bartering and merits since nobody wants a reminder of the potential fate in store for them represented by that tower

I’m not hard stuck to calling them scler, I wanted to come up with something tying back to the eye symbolism. The name comes from sclera, the area surrounding the iris. Open to new names if a better one is suggested. 
Scler is intended to be both the singular and plural version. To alleviate the need to carry around several smaller value ones, I imagine a 10, 20, 50, and 100 coin exist.

Owing to its coastal geography, Nervios has large quantities of fish incorporated into its food trade, with vessels used long ago to travel shallow depths carrying goods to other nations being stripped of their cannons in favor of nets and harpoons, equipped to sail the open ocean should the need arise. Fields on the outskirts of Nervios supply the grains and fruits comprising the remaining portion of the market.

Traditional farms / fields could be swapped out as a concept for gardens, or weaving nature into architecture (planters in the sides and walls of buildings, rooftop plots, etc…)
Think the economy would naturally split 60/40 fish to non-fish food sources unless we radically lean into green architecture

Practically none of the common folk can afford to recover from disaster of any nature, families without a means to earn the scul needed to scrape by simply won’t last. Because of this, many households stricken by illness or death suffer  downward spirals, causing an all too common exchange of remaining valuables to avoid or recover from complete destitution.
